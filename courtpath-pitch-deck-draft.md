# CourtPath pitch deck draft

## Slide 1 — Title
**CourtPath**  
A trusted court-navigation and legal-workflow platform for people, families, and organizations handling court-related matters.

## Slide 2 — Problem
Millions of people face court processes without affordable, timely legal help, and the experience is fragmented across forms, deadlines, websites, court rules, filing systems, and inconsistent guidance.[cite:323][cite:319] For self-represented litigants and small organizations, the result is missed steps, wasted money, procedural mistakes, and overwhelming stress.[cite:323]

## Slide 3 — Why now
Courts, filing workflows, and legal information have become more digital, while AI has made guided assistance more accessible to ordinary users.[cite:323][cite:326] At the same time, investors increasingly expect startups to explain why the timing is right, making “why now” a critical part of the story.[cite:326][cite:320]

## Slide 4 — Solution
CourtPath gives users a structured path through court-related work: guided intake, matter setup, document readiness, filing preparation, deadline tracking, and escalation to human support when needed.[cite:323][cite:319] Instead of acting like a black-box legal bot, it is designed as a trust-centered workflow product grounded in clear next steps and transparent source handling.[cite:323]

## Slide 5 — Product
Core product modules include:
- Guided intake and issue organization.
- Matter dashboard and event timeline.
- Document and filing readiness workflow.
- Alerts, deadlines, and next-action engine.
- Human-help routing for legal aid, service partners, and professionals.

Investors typically expect the product slide to make the core features and user value immediately legible without overloading detail.[cite:323][cite:319]

## Slide 6 — How it works
The user starts with a situation, not a legal form.[cite:323] CourtPath turns that situation into a matter workspace, organizes events and documents, identifies missing steps, and guides the user toward completion, escalation, or filing preparation.[cite:323][cite:326] This product flow keeps the pitch grounded in user outcomes, which is a standard best practice for concise investor storytelling.[cite:324]

## Slide 7 — Users
CourtPath serves two connected user groups:
- Public users, including self-represented individuals, families, and small business operators.
- Professional or institutional users, including nonprofits, intake teams, legal-aid staff, and partner organizations.

A clear customer definition helps investors understand adoption, pricing, and go-to-market logic.[cite:323][cite:327]

## Slide 8 — Market opportunity
The opportunity is large because court navigation, legal intake, document preparation, workflow support, and self-service legal help all sit inside a broad and persistent access-to-justice problem.[cite:323][cite:327] Investors generally expect market slides to connect a big top-down opportunity with a more credible serviceable market tied to the actual launch model.[cite:323][cite:326]

## Slide 9 — Why CourtPath wins
CourtPath is differentiated by four design choices:
- Trust-centered workflow instead of generic chatbot behavior.
- Dual-audience design for both public users and organizations.
- Matter-centered workspace rather than isolated documents.
- Clear escalation path to human support instead of pretending AI can replace lawyers.

Competition slides and differentiation slides are standard because investors want to know why a startup will win and how it is meaningfully different.[cite:319][cite:323]

## Slide 10 — Business model
Potential revenue streams include:
- Consumer subscription tiers for advanced workflow and document support.
- Organization and partner seats for intake, collaboration, and matter management.
- Premium workflow services, referral-enabled handoffs, or enterprise partnerships where allowed.

Investors want the business model explained clearly, especially how the company makes money and how distribution connects to revenue.[cite:323][cite:319]

## Slide 11 — Go-to-market
The launch strategy should begin with an Illinois-first or jurisdiction-first wedge, then expand through user demand, legal-aid partnerships, community channels, and institutional relationships.[cite:323] Go-to-market belongs in the deck because investors want to see not only a good product idea, but a believable path to adoption.[cite:319][cite:323]

## Slide 12 — Validation and traction
Early traction can include prototype progress, user interviews, waitlist interest, workflow testing, advisor support, and pilot discussions.[cite:323][cite:325] Investors expect more than vision alone; even early-stage traction slides should demonstrate that assumptions are being tested and that real demand signals exist.[cite:325][cite:322]

## Slide 13 — Roadmap
Recommended roadmap:
- Phase 1: CourtPath MVP for guided intake, matter timeline, alerts, document readiness, and trusted-source workflows.
- Phase 2: Partner mode for nonprofits and legal-service organizations.
- Phase 3: Multi-jurisdiction rollout, richer automations, and stronger referral infrastructure.

Roadmap slides help investors understand what capital unlocks and what milestones should be achieved before the next raise.[cite:323][cite:326]

## Slide 14 — Team
The team slide should show why this company has founder-market fit: deep firsthand exposure to the pain, strong product conviction, and the willingness to build a serious trust-centered platform in a difficult category.[cite:323] Investors care about why this team is suited to solve the problem, especially in complex and regulated sectors.[cite:323][cite:328]

## Slide 15 — Ask
CourtPath is seeking capital, advisors, pilot partners, and implementation support to move from sophisticated prototype to live pilot and measured market launch.[cite:323][cite:326] The ask slide should make clear what is needed, what it will fund, and what milestones it is intended to achieve.[cite:323]

## Presentation notes
The best pitch decks stay clear, concise, and story-driven rather than overloaded with text.[cite:323][cite:324] This draft should therefore be turned into a visual slide deck with short headlines, 3-5 bullets per slide, clean screenshots or product mockups, and one central idea per page.[cite:323][cite:326]
