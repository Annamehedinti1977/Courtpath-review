When court processes feel impossible, people fall behind fast

Most people do not start with the right form, the right sequence, or a clean timeline.

They start with stress, scattered paperwork, unclear deadlines, and too many places to search for answers. CourtPath is built for that reality.

  • People need structure before they need sophistication.
  • Deadline pressure makes disconnected information harder to use.
  • Support organizations need cleaner intake, handoff, and review workflows.
Why structure matters
Paperwork arrives
The person does not yet know what is official, urgent, or optional.
Deadlines stay unclear
Missed steps often come from disorganization, not lack of effort.
Search fragments the process
Forms, portals, instructions, and support options live in different places.
Higher risk goes unseen
Users need visible off-ramps to legal aid, attorneys, or staff review.

Audience

Pilot positioning

Designed to start narrow, prove trust, and expand deliberately.

The strongest first release is Illinois-first and workflow-first, with one production-grade matter path rather than broad national coverage.
