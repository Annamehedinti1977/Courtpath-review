# CourtPath landing page copy

## Hero
**Headline:** Court guidance that helps people get organized before they fall behind.  
**Subheadline:** CourtPath helps individuals, families, small businesses, and support organizations turn confusing court-related work into clear next steps, organized documents, and a better path to filing readiness.[cite:338][cite:340]

**Primary CTA:** Join the pilot  
**Microcopy:** See how CourtPath can support court workflows without replacing human legal judgment.[cite:309][cite:338]

A high-converting landing page should lead with a clear value proposition and one primary CTA above the fold.[cite:338][cite:309][cite:340]

## Trust section
**Heading:** Built for trust, not black-box answers.  
**Copy:** CourtPath is designed around guided workflows, visible next steps, and support escalation when the matter needs human attention. It is built to help users organize court-related work more clearly and reduce the risk of missed steps.[cite:340][cite:309]

Trust signals near the conversion area help reduce perceived risk and improve action rates.[cite:309][cite:340][cite:345]

## Problem section
**Heading:** When court processes feel impossible, people fall behind fast.  
**Copy:** Most people do not start with the right form, the right filing sequence, or a clean timeline of events. They start with stress, scattered paperwork, unclear deadlines, and too many places to search for answers. CourtPath is built for that reality.[cite:340][cite:345]

Landing page copy performs better when it speaks directly to user pain points and benefits instead of listing features too early.[cite:340][cite:345]

## How it works
**Heading:** A clearer path through court-related work.  
**Step 1:** Start with your situation in plain language.  
**Step 2:** Build one organized matter workspace with tasks, files, and timeline events.  
**Step 3:** Follow guided next steps toward document readiness, filing preparation, and human-help routing when needed.[cite:309][cite:338]

Good landing pages make the path to value easy to scan and easy to understand on mobile screens.[cite:309][cite:345]

## Audience section
**Heading:** Built for the public and the professionals who support them.  
**Copy:** CourtPath is designed for self-represented users, families, and small businesses, but also for nonprofits, legal-service organizations, intake teams, and support staff who help people navigate complicated processes.[cite:340][cite:345]

Clear audience positioning improves relevance and helps visitors understand whether the product is meant for them.[cite:340]

## Feature highlights
- Guided intake that starts with the user’s situation, not legal jargon.[cite:340]
- Matter dashboard with deadlines, tasks, and progress visibility.[cite:340]
- Timeline and document organization in one place.[cite:340]
- Filing-readiness support and next-step prioritization.[cite:340]
- Human-help escalation when the situation becomes higher risk.[cite:345]

## CTA block
**Heading:** Bring order to the next court-related matter before it gets harder.  
**CTA:** Join the pilot  
**Support line:** For early users, partner organizations, and teams exploring structured court-workflow support.[cite:309][cite:338]

Forms should stay simple and focused, usually with only the essential fields needed for the primary conversion action.[cite:309][cite:338]

## Suggested form fields
- Name
- Email
- I am interested as: individual, organization, legal-service team, or partner

Short forms generally convert better because they reduce friction and keep the next step obvious.[cite:309][cite:338]

## Messaging rules
- Keep one clear CTA throughout the page.[cite:338][cite:309]
- Use clear, benefit-led language instead of vague startup phrasing.[cite:340][cite:345]
- Put trust signals close to the CTA.[cite:309][cite:340]
- Keep the page mobile-friendly, fast, and visually clean.[cite:309]
- Use headlines and subheads that are easy to scan.[cite:345]
